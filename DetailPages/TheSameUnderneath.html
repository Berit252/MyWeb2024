The Same Underneath

这是一个以自主神经系统结构为灵感的装置作品。
This is an installation using ANS's structure as inspiration.

当我们将地球视为一个开放的生物场，将人类与其他动物放在同一基础上进行比较，我们能发现相似之处吗？如果我们对自己更诚实一点，什么时候才能发现人的兽性呢？带着这些问题，我作为一个有机体回顾了自己，回忆起自己在日常生活中的主观感受，经历着不受意识控制的内心反应。从那时起我了解了自主神经系统（又名植物神经系统）。它控制着我重要的内脏和感觉，而我的意识却常常忽视它的存在，就像高等动物常常忘记它们也是地球生物一样。我的好奇心促使我深入研究了这个神经系统。
As we look at the Earth as an open biofield, comparing humans to other animals on the same footing, can we find any similarities? If we are more honest with ourselves, when will the animality of human can be detected? With these questions, I as an organism looked back at myself, recalling my subjective feelings in my daily life and experiencing internal reactions that were not controlled by my consciousness. I learned about the Autonomic Nervous System (aka Vegetative Nervous System) from this point. It controls my vital internal organs and sensibilities, while my consciousness often ignores its existence, just like higher animals often forget that they are also one of the earth’s creatures. My curiosity led me to delve deeper into this nervous system and conduct a comprehensive study.

与躯体自主神经反射不同，自主神经从脊髓出来后需要被神经节取代。这使得它的工作速度更慢，但范围更广。神经节是多个神经元汇聚的产物，在显微镜下是一堆神经节细胞被一群卫星胶质细胞紧密包围的图像。乍一看，它们可能看起来很混乱，但它们可以帮助我们分析难以用理性理解的感官信号。
Unlike the somatic-autonomic reflex, autonomic nerves need to be replaced by ganglions after coming out of the spinal cord. This makes it work slower, but more extensive. The ganglion is the product of the convergence of multiple neurons, and under the microscope is the image of a bunch of ganglion cells closely surrounded by a group of satellite glial cells. At first glance, they may seem chaotic, but they help us analyze sensory signals that are difficult to understand rationally.
